\chapternonum{摘要}

水稻是全球最重要的粮食作物之一，供养了世界一半以上的人口，其种植区的时空动态变化对全球粮食安全和环境系统都具有深远影响。
然而，全球水稻种植表现出显著的时空异质性，不同稻区的种植期不固定、生长季长度差异大，给全球尺度的水稻遥感制图带来了巨大挑战 \cite{Dong2016}。
准确掌握全球水稻的时空分布格局是评估其环境效应的基础数据支撑，但现有数据产品难以同时满足全球覆盖、日尺度动态、长时序连续和中等空间分辨率的多维需求 \cite{Xiao2006}。
在稻田的气候效应研究方面，目前已有研究更侧重于生物化学升温效应（即甲烷排放导致的大气增温），而对地表生物物理降温效应的系统认识仍显不足，缺乏基于真实水稻生长季的全球尺度量化评估。

针对上述问题，本文提出融合物候先验知识与动态时间规整（Dynamic Time Warping, DTW）曲线匹配策略的 MPD\_DTW 框架，实现全球不同气候背景、地理条件和种植制度下水稻生长季的自动识别。
该框架将水稻特有的多阶段物候特征嵌入 DTW 匹配过程，在不依赖区域特定作物日历的前提下，自适应地识别水稻生长季的起止时间，兼具空间可迁移性和时间适应性。
基于该框架，本文构建 2001--2022 年全球 500 m 日尺度水稻种植区数据集 GlobalRice500，涵盖全球主要稻区，附带逐像元生长季起始日（SOS）和结束日（EOS）信息。GlobalRice500 揭示了全球水稻面积整体呈波动上升趋势，22 年间净增加超过 2100 万公顷，亚洲长期占全球水稻面积的 85\% 以上，非洲是增长最快的大洲。

基于 GlobalRice500 识别的真实水稻生长季，本文首次系统评估了全球稻田地表温度（LST）相对于周边非稻田区域的差异，揭示稻田在生长季白天具有广泛的局地降温效应。研究结果表明，全球稻田白天平均降温约 0.21--0.27 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$，降温强度在分蘖--抽穗期达到峰值，且夜间 $\Delta$LST 约为 0.01--0.02 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$，虽显著大于 0 但量级远小于白天，进一步证实了降温机制主要源于白天太阳辐射驱动下的蒸散发过程 \cite{Li2021}。进一步分析发现，降温强度受稻田比例、田块规模和空间外溢效应的显著调控：稻田比例每增加 1\%，降温幅度约增加 0.0014 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$；大田块的平均降温幅度可达 0.69 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$，显著高于小田块的 0.18 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$；稻田降温效应不仅局限于稻田内部，还向周边非稻田区域产生明显的空间外溢。本研究揭示了稻田气候效应中长期被忽视的生物物理降温维度，为全面认识稻田对气候系统的双重影响提供了新的科学证据 \cite{Weng2025}。

综上所述，全球稻田动态制图是认识稻田气候调节效应的基础数据支撑。本研究在方法层面提出了兼具普适性和精度的 MPD\_DTW 识别框架，在数据层面构建了长时序日尺度的 GlobalRice500 全球水稻数据集，在气候效应层面首次系统揭示了全球稻田地表降温效应的时空格局及其调控机制。研究成果为农业土地利用变化的气候反馈研究、农业景观规划和可持续水稻种植管理提供了重要的科学依据和数据参考。

\chapternonum{Abstract}

Rice is one of the most important food crops globally, sustaining more than half of the world's population, and the spatiotemporal dynamics of rice cultivation areas have profound implications for global food security and the climate system. However, global rice cultivation exhibits significant spatiotemporal heterogeneity, with inconsistent planting dates and varying growing-season lengths across regions, posing substantial challenges to global-scale rice mapping using remote sensing \cite{Dong2016}. Accurate dynamic mapping of global paddy rice is the fundamental data basis for assessing its climate regulation effects, yet existing datasets struggle to simultaneously satisfy the needs of global coverage, daily temporal resolution, long-term continuity, and moderate spatial resolution \cite{Xiao2006}. In particular, regarding the climate effects of rice paddies, previous studies have predominantly focused on the warming effect of methane emissions, while the systematic understanding of surface biophysical cooling effects remains insufficient, lacking global-scale quantitative assessments based on actual rice growing seasons.

To address these challenges, this study proposes the MPD\_DTW framework that integrates phenological prior knowledge with dynamic time warping (DTW) curve matching to automatically identify rice growing seasons under diverse cropping systems worldwide. By embedding the multi-stage phenological characteristics unique to rice into the DTW matching process, this framework adaptively detects the start and end dates of rice growing seasons without relying on region-specific thresholds, demonstrating both spatial transferability and temporal adaptability. Based on this framework, we develop GlobalRice500, a global 500-m daily rice planting area dataset covering 2001--2022, encompassing major rice-producing regions worldwide with pixel-level start-of-season (SOS) and end-of-season (EOS) information. GlobalRice500 reveals that global rice area exhibited an overall fluctuating upward trend during the study period, with a net increase of more than 21 million hectares over the 22 years. Asia consistently accounted for over 85\% of global rice area, while Africa emerged as the fastest-growing continent in terms of rice cultivation expansion.

Utilizing the actual growing seasons identified by GlobalRice500, this study provides the first systematic assessment of the differences in land surface temperature (LST) between paddy rice and surrounding non-rice areas, revealing a widespread daytime local cooling effect during the rice growing season. The results indicate that global rice paddies exhibit an average daytime cooling of approximately 0.21--0.27 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$, with peak cooling intensity occurring during the tillering--heading stage. The nighttime $\Delta$LST of approximately 0.01--0.02 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$, though significantly greater than zero, is far smaller than the daytime cooling effect, further confirming that the cooling mechanism is primarily driven by evapotranspiration under solar radiation during daytime \cite{Li2021}. Further analyses demonstrate that cooling intensity is significantly regulated by paddy proportion, field size, and spatial spillover effects: each 1\% increase in paddy proportion corresponds to an approximately 0.0014 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ enhancement in cooling magnitude; large fields (size 11) exhibit an average cooling of approximately 0.69 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$, substantially higher than the 0.18 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ observed for small fields (size 1); moreover, the cooling effect extends beyond paddy field boundaries, generating notable spatial spillover into adjacent non-rice areas. This study fills a critical knowledge gap by revealing the biophysical cooling dimension of rice paddy climate effects, providing new scientific evidence for comprehensively understanding the dual impacts of rice cultivation on the climate system \cite{Weng2025}.

In summary, dynamic global rice mapping serves as the essential data foundation for understanding the climate regulation effects of rice paddies. This study contributes methodologically by proposing the MPD\_DTW framework with both universal applicability and high accuracy, contributes to data infrastructure by constructing the long-term daily GlobalRice500 global rice dataset, and contributes to climate science by systematically revealing the spatiotemporal patterns and regulatory mechanisms of global rice paddy surface cooling effects. The research findings provide important scientific evidence and data references for studies on climate feedbacks of agricultural land-use change, agricultural landscape planning, and sustainable rice cultivation management.
