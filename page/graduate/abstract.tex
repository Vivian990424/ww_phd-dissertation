\chapternonum{摘要}
% 背景
水稻种植区是全球农业土地系统中高度集约利用且受人为水分管理强烈影响的重要类型，其空间分布与生长季动态不仅关系到全球粮食供给格局，也直接影响农业水资源消耗、稻田温室气体排放和地表能量分配过程。
已有稻田气候效应研究长期关注甲烷排放及其生物地球化学增温作用，但稻田对气候和地表热环境的影响并不局限于温室气体排放。
作为典型的水—土壤—植被复合下垫面，稻田在水稻生长季内通过田面水层蒸发和水稻冠层蒸腾增强潜热通量、调节显热交换并改变地表能量分配，从而可能对地表温度产生生物物理调节作用。
相较于甲烷排放研究，稻田地表温度效应在全球尺度上的定量认识仍较为有限，关键制约在于缺乏能够同时表征全球覆盖、长期连续、中等空间分辨率和逐像元生长季动态的水稻数据。
该数据缺口与水稻种植系统自身的复杂性密切相关：全球水稻生产受气候背景、水分管理、播栽制度、品种熟期和复种方式共同影响，表现出播栽期不固定、生长季长度差异大、年内多季种植普遍和遥感时序信号复杂等特点，使跨区域一致的全球水稻动态识别面临显著挑战。

% 水稻提取
针对全球复杂稻作制度下水稻真实生长季难以统一识别的问题，本文提出融合移动物候检测与动态时间规整的 MPD\_DTW 框架。该框架基于 MODIS Terra/Aqua 地表反射率重建的逐日 NDVI 和 LSWI 时间序列，综合利用水稻移栽期的淹水信号、收获期绿度衰退特征以及生长季内植被指数曲线形态，逐像元逐日连续识别水稻生长季起始日和结束日，并通过动态时间规整判断候选生长季是否符合水稻生长过程。
基于该框架，本文构建了 2001--2022 年全球 500 m 逐日水稻动态数据集 GlobalRice500，提供全球水稻空间分布、逐像元生长季起止时间和年内种植季次信息。
结合全球典型参考区高分辨率水稻图、目视解译样本和 FAO 国家尺度统计资料等多源参考资料，本文从像元级空间识别精度和国家尺度面积一致性两个层面对数据集可靠性进行了评价。
结果表明，GlobalRice500 在全球九个典型参考区的总体精度为 88.85\%--99.60\%，与 113 个国家 2001--2022 年 FAO 水稻统计面积保持高度一致，能够支撑全球、洲际和国家尺度的长期水稻时空动态分析。
2001--2022 年，全球水稻收获面积由约 1.38 亿公顷增加至约 1.59 亿公顷，净增加超过 2100 万公顷。
亚洲始终是全球水稻面积的主体，占比长期超过 85\%，并贡献了全球净增长的 70.7\%；非洲水稻面积由约 770 万公顷增加至约 1540 万公顷，成为增长最快的大洲，其正向增加相当于全球净增长的 39.4\%。
国家尺度上，印度和中国继续构成全球水稻种植分布格局的双核心；尼日利亚、柬埔寨等国家的水稻面积增长和位序上升，表明全球水稻扩张正在向部分非洲和东南亚新兴稻区延伸。

% 温度
基于 GlobalRice500 提供的水稻空间分布和生长季信息，本文进一步结合 2003--2020 年全球 1 km 日尺度地表温度（LST）数据，构建稻田与周边非水稻农田的对照分析框架，并将非水稻农田 LST 与稻田 LST 之差定义为 $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$，以量化稻田相对于邻近农田的地表温度效应。
结果表明，全球稻田在水稻生长季内具有广泛而稳定的日间局地地表降温效应，年均 $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ 为 0.21--0.27~$^\circ$C，且各年份 95\% 置信区间均高于 0；夜间 $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ 接近 0，表明稻田降温主要发生在太阳辐射驱动下蒸散发增强和显热通量降低的日间能量分配过程中。
稻田降温效应具有明确的物候阶段性，日间 $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ 随水稻生长季推进呈先增强后减弱的“倒 V 型”变化，并在 DOS 60--100 附近达到峰值，对应水稻冠层快速发育和蒸散发活跃阶段。
进一步分析表明，稻田地表降温效应受到景观组成、田块空间连续性和边界邻近关系的共同调控。
局地稻田比例每增加 1\%，日间 $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ 平均增加约 0.0014~$^\circ$C；
随着田块尺度由约 0.5~km 增至约 5.5~km，日间平均降温由约 0.13~$^\circ$C 增至约 0.43~$^\circ$C，生长季内峰值降温由约 0.18~$^\circ$C 增至约 0.69~$^\circ$C。
基于非水稻农田梯度环带的分析显示，稻田低温信号可向邻近非水稻农田扩展，并随距稻田边界距离增加逐渐减弱。
上述结果表明，稻田降温是田面水分、冠层发育和景观结构共同作用下形成的地表生物物理效应，具有显著的时间阶段性、尺度依赖性和空间外溢特征。

%总结意义
本研究形成了从全球水稻生长季事件识别、全球长期水稻数据集构建到稻田地表气候效应评估的完整研究链条。
MPD\_DTW 为跨区域、跨制度水稻动态遥感监测提供了具有物候解释性的技术框架；
GlobalRice500 为全球水稻分布、种植季次和生长季过程研究提供了统一数据基础；
稻田地表降温效应评估揭示了水稻生态系统除温室气体排放之外对地表热环境的生物物理调节作用。
本研究将稻田从静态作物分布对象拓展为具有可识别生长季过程和可量化地表气候效应的动态土地系统单元，有助于在粮食生产、水资源利用、温室气体排放和局地热环境调节之间建立更加完整的稻田生态系统气候效应认识，并为农业土地系统的可持续管理和气候适应研究提供数据基础与科学依据。

\chapternonum{Abstract}

Rice cultivation areas are an important type of highly intensive land use in the global agricultural land system and are strongly shaped by anthropogenic water management. Their spatial distribution and growing-season dynamics are not only closely related to global food supply patterns, but also directly influence agricultural water consumption, greenhouse gas emissions from paddy fields, and land surface energy partitioning.
Previous studies on the climate effects of paddy fields have long focused on methane emissions and their biogeochemical warming effects. However, the impacts of paddy fields on climate and the land surface thermal environment are not limited to greenhouse gas emissions.
As a typical coupled water--soil--vegetation land surface, paddy fields enhance latent heat flux, regulate sensible heat exchange, and alter surface energy partitioning during the rice growing season through evaporation from standing water and transpiration from rice canopies, thereby potentially exerting a biophysical regulatory effect on land surface temperature.
Compared with methane emission studies, quantitative understanding of the land surface temperature effect of paddy fields at the global scale remains limited. A key constraint is the lack of rice datasets that simultaneously provide global coverage, long-term continuity, moderate spatial resolution, and pixel-level growing-season dynamics.
This data gap is closely associated with the intrinsic complexity of rice cultivation systems. Global rice production is jointly influenced by climatic background, water management, planting systems, cultivar duration, and multiple-cropping practices, leading to variable planting dates, diverse growing-season lengths, widespread within-year multiple rice seasons, and complex remote sensing temporal signals. These characteristics pose substantial challenges for consistent global dynamic rice identification across regions.

% Rice mapping
To address the difficulty of consistently identifying actual rice growing seasons under complex global rice cropping systems, this study proposes the MPD\_DTW framework by integrating Moving Phenological Detection with Dynamic Time Warping.
Based on daily NDVI and LSWI time series reconstructed from MODIS Terra/Aqua surface reflectance data, this framework jointly uses flooding signals during the rice transplanting period, greenness decline during harvest, and vegetation-index curve shapes within the growing season to continuously identify the start and end dates of rice growing seasons at the pixel and daily scales. Dynamic Time Warping is then used to determine whether each candidate growing season conforms to the rice growth process.
Based on this framework, this study developed GlobalRice500, a global 500~m daily rice dynamics dataset for 2001--2022, which provides global rice spatial distribution, pixel-level growing-season start and end dates, and within-year rice-season frequency.
Using multiple reference sources, including high-resolution rice maps from representative global reference regions, visually interpreted samples, and FAO national-scale statistics, the reliability of GlobalRice500 was evaluated from two perspectives: pixel-level spatial identification accuracy and national-scale area consistency.
The results show that GlobalRice500 achieved overall accuracies of 88.85\%--99.60\% across nine representative global reference regions and showed high consistency with FAO rice statistical areas for 113 countries during 2001--2022, indicating its capacity to support long-term analyses of rice spatiotemporal dynamics at global, continental, and national scales.
From 2001 to 2022, the global harvested rice area increased from approximately 138 million hectares to 159 million hectares, with a net increase of more than 21 million hectares.
Asia remained the dominant contributor to global rice area, accounting for more than 85\% throughout the study period and contributing 70.7\% of the global net increase. Rice area in Africa increased from approximately 7.7 million hectares to 15.4 million hectares, making it the fastest-growing continent, with its positive increase equivalent to 39.4\% of the global net increase.
At the national scale, India and China continued to form the two core centers of the global rice cultivation distribution pattern. The expansion and increasing ranks of rice area in countries such as Nigeria and Cambodia indicate that global rice expansion is extending toward emerging rice-growing regions in parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

% Temperature
Based on the rice spatial distribution and growing-season information provided by GlobalRice500, this study further combined a global 1~km daily land surface temperature (LST) dataset for 2003--2020 to establish a comparative analytical framework between paddy fields and surrounding non-rice croplands. The difference between non-rice cropland LST and paddy-field LST was defined as $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ to quantify the land surface temperature effect of paddy fields relative to adjacent croplands.
The results show that global paddy fields exhibit a widespread and stable daytime local land surface cooling effect during the rice growing season, with annual mean $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ ranging from 0.21 to 0.27~$^\circ$C and 95\% confidence intervals above zero in all years. Nighttime $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ is close to zero, indicating that paddy cooling mainly occurs during daytime energy partitioning under solar-radiation forcing, through enhanced evapotranspiration and reduced sensible heat flux.
The paddy-field cooling effect shows clear phenological stage dependence. Daytime $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ follows an inverted V-shaped pattern as the rice growing season progresses, increasing first and then decreasing, and reaches its peak around DOS 60--100, corresponding to the stage of rapid rice canopy development and active evapotranspiration.
Further analyses indicate that the land surface cooling effect of paddy fields is jointly regulated by landscape composition, spatial continuity of paddy patches, and boundary proximity.
For each 1\% increase in local paddy proportion, daytime $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ increases by approximately 0.0014~$^\circ$C on average.
As paddy-field scale increases from approximately 0.5~km to 5.5~km, mean daytime cooling increases from approximately 0.13~$^\circ$C to 0.43~$^\circ$C, and peak cooling within the growing season increases from approximately 0.18~$^\circ$C to 0.69~$^\circ$C.
The analysis based on non-rice cropland gradient rings shows that the cool surface signal of paddy fields can extend into adjacent non-rice croplands and gradually weakens with increasing distance from paddy-field boundaries.
These results indicate that paddy cooling is a land surface biophysical effect formed by the joint influence of standing water, canopy development, and landscape structure, with significant temporal stage dependence, scale dependence, and spatial spillover characteristics.
