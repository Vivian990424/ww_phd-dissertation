\chapternonum{摘要}
% 背景
稻田是全球农业土地系统中高度集约利用且受人为水分管理深刻影响的重要类型，其空间分布与生长季动态不仅关系到全球粮食供给格局，也影响农业水资源消耗、稻田温室气体排放和地表能量分配过程。
已有稻田气候效应研究长期关注甲烷排放及其生物地球化学增温作用，对稻田通过地表能量平衡调节地表热环境的生物物理效应认识仍不充分。
作为典型的水—土壤—植被复合下垫面，稻田在水稻生长季内通过田面水层蒸发和水稻冠层蒸腾增强潜热通量、调节显热交换并改变地表能量分配，进而可能形成可观测的地表降温效应。
全球尺度稻田地表温度效应研究的关键制约在于，现有水稻数据难以同时满足全球覆盖、长期连续、中等空间分辨率和逐日逐像元等需求。
这一数据缺口与水稻种植系统自身的复杂性密切相关：全球水稻生产受气候背景、水分管理、播栽制度、品种熟期和复种方式共同影响，表现出播栽期不固定、生长季长度差异大、年内种植季次差异显著和遥感时序信号复杂等特征，使跨区域一致的全球水稻种植区提取面临显著挑战。

% 水稻提取
针对全球复杂稻作制度下水稻真实生长季难以统一识别的问题，本文提出融合移动物候检测与动态时间规整的 MPD\_DTW 框架。
该框架由移动物候检测（Moving Phenological Detection, MPD）和动态时间规整（Dynamic Time Warping, DTW）两个相互协作的模块构成。
MPD 模块基于 MODIS Terra/Aqua 地表反射率重建的逐日归一化植被指数（normalized difference vegetation index, NDVI）和陆地表面水分指数（land surface water index, LSWI）时间序列，综合利用水稻移栽期的淹水信号、收获期绿度衰退特征以及生长季内植被指数曲线形态，逐像元、逐日识别潜在的水稻生长季起始日和结束日。
DTW 模块将继续计算候选生长季 NDVI 曲线与标准水稻曲线的相似性。对于同一候选 SOS 下的多个候选 EOS，选取 DTW 距离最小的 SOS--EOS 片段作为该候选 SOS 对应的最优候选生长季。
该处理使水稻生长季筛选同时受到物候知识和完整生长曲线形态的约束，有助于降低仅凭物候规则确定生长季起止点所导致的不稳定性。

% GlobalRice500
基于该框架，本文构建了 2001--2022 年全球 500 m 逐日水稻种植区数据集 GlobalRice500。
结合全球典型参考区高分辨率水稻图、目视解译样本和联合国粮食及农业组织（Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations, FAO）国家尺度统计资料等多源参考资料，本文从像元级空间识别精度和国家尺度面积一致性两个层面对数据集可靠性进行了评价。
结果表明，GlobalRice500 在全球九个典型参考区的总体精度为 88.85\%--99.60\%，与 113 个国家 2001--2022 年 FAO 水稻统计面积保持高度一致，能够支撑全球、洲际和国家尺度的长期水稻时空动态分析。
基于 GlobalRice500，本文进一步揭示了 21 世纪以来全球水稻种植格局的变化。
2001--2022 年，全球水稻收获面积由约 1.38 亿公顷增加至约 1.59 亿公顷，净增加超过 2100 万公顷。
亚洲始终是全球水稻收获面积的主体，占比长期超过 85\%，并贡献了全球净增长的 70.7\%；非洲水稻面积由约 770 万公顷增加至约 1540 万公顷，为增长最快的大洲，其正向增长量相当于全球净增加量的 39.4\%。
国家尺度上，印度和中国继续构成全球水稻种植分布格局的双核心；尼日利亚、柬埔寨等国家的水稻面积增长和位序上升，表明全球水稻扩张正在向部分非洲和东南亚新兴稻区延伸。、

% 温度
在全球长时序逐日水稻种植区提取的基础上，本文进一步结合 2003--2020 年全球 1 km 日尺度地表温度（Land Surface Temperature，LST）数据，构建稻田与周边非水稻农田的对照分析框架，并将非水稻农田 LST 与稻田 LST 之差定义为 $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$，用于量化稻田相对于邻近农田的地表温度效应。
结果表明，全球稻田在水稻生长季内具有广泛而稳定的日间局地地表降温效应，年均 $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ 为 0.21--0.27~$^\circ$C，且各年份 95\% 置信区间均高于 0。
夜间 $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ 量级远小于日间并接近 0，表明稻田降温主要发生在太阳辐射驱动下蒸散发增强和显热通量降低的日间能量分配过程中。
稻田降温效应具有明确的物候阶段性，日间 $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ 随水稻生长季推进呈先增强后减弱的“倒 V 型”变化，并在生长季日序（day of season, DOS）60--100 附近达到峰值，对应水稻冠层快速发育和蒸散发活跃阶段。
进一步分析表明，稻田地表降温效应受到景观组成、田块空间连续性和边界邻近关系的共同调控。
在 10 km 网格尺度上，局地稻田比例每增加 1\%，日间 $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ 平均增加约 0.0014~$^\circ$C。
随着稻田田块尺度由约 0.5~km 增至约 5.5~km，日间平均降温由约 0.13~$^\circ$C 增至约 0.43~$^\circ$C，生长季内峰值降温由约 0.18~$^\circ$C 增至约 0.69~$^\circ$C。
基于非水稻农田梯度环带的分析显示，稻田低温信号可向邻近非水稻农田扩展，并随距稻田边界距离增加逐渐减弱。
上述结果表明，稻田降温是在田面水分、冠层发育和景观结构共同作用下形成的地表生物物理效应，具有显著的时间阶段性、尺度依赖性和空间外溢特征。

%总结意义
本研究形成了从逐日水稻生长季识别算法开发、全球长时序逐日水稻种植区数据集构建到全球稻田地表气候效应评估的完整研究链条。
MPD\_DTW 为跨区域、跨制度逐日水稻种植区提供了具有物候解释性的技术框架；
GlobalRice500 将该识别能力转化为长期、连续、可复用的全球水稻动态数据基础，并为真实生长季约束下的稻田地表温度效应评估提供了关键支撑；
稻田地表降温效应评估揭示了水稻生态系统除温室气体排放之外对地表热环境的生物物理调节作用。
本研究将稻田从静态作物分布对象拓展为具有可识别生长季过程和可量化地表气候效应的动态土地系统单元，有助于在粮食生产、水资源利用、温室气体排放和局地热环境调节之间建立更加完整的稻田生态系统气候效应认识，并为农业土地系统的可持续管理和气候适应研究提供数据基础与科学依据。

\chapternonum{Abstract}

Paddy fields are an important land use type of highly intensified agricultural land system at the global scale and are profoundly influenced by anthropogenic water management. Their spatial distribution and growing-season dynamics are not only related to the global pattern of food supply, but also affect agricultural water-resource consumption, greenhouse gas emissions from paddy fields, and land-surface energy partitioning. 
Existing studies on the climatic effects of paddy fields have long focused on methane emissions and their biogeochemical warming effects, whereas the biophysical effects of paddy fields in regulating the land-surface thermal environment through the surface energy balance remain insufficiently understood. 
As a typical water--soil--vegetation composite underlying surface, paddy fields enhance latent heat flux through evaporation from the surface water layer and transpiration from the rice canopy during the rice growing season, regulate sensible heat exchange, and alter land-surface energy partitioning, thereby potentially producing an observable land-surface cooling effect. 
A key constraint on global-scale studies of the land surface temperature effects of paddy fields is that existing rice datasets can hardly meet, at the same time, the requirements of global coverage, long-term continuity, moderate spatial resolution, and daily pixel-level characterization. 
This data gap is closely related to the complexity of rice cropping systems themselves. Global rice production is jointly influenced by climatic background, water management, sowing and transplanting systems, cultivar maturity, and multiple-cropping practices, resulting in variable planting dates, diverse growing-season lengths, variations in the number of intra-annual cropping seasons, and complex remote-sensing time-series signals. These characteristics pose substantial challenges to the cross-regionally consistent extraction of global rice planting areas.

% Rice mapping
To address the difficulty of uniformly identifying actual rice growing seasons under globally complex rice cropping systems, this study proposes an MPD\_DTW framework that integrates Moving Phenological Detection and Dynamic Time Warping.
The framework consists of two collaborative modules: Moving Phenological Detection (MPD) and Dynamic Time Warping (DTW). 
Based on daily normalized difference vegetation index (NDVI) and land surface water index (LSWI) time series reconstructed from MODIS Terra/Aqua surface reflectance data, the MPD module integrates the flooding signal during the rice transplanting stage, the greenness decline feature during the harvest stage, and the overall shape of vegetation-index trajectories within the growing season. It identifies potential start-of-season (SOS) and end-of-season (EOS) dates for rice on a pixel-by-pixel and day-by-day basis.
The DTW module then calculates the similarity between the NDVI trajectory of each candidate growing season and a standard rice growth curve. For multiple candidate EOS dates associated with the same candidate SOS, the SOS--EOS segment with the smallest DTW distance is selected as the optimal candidate growing season corresponding to that SOS.
This framework constrains rice growing-season selection using both phenological knowledge and the complete shape of the seasonal growth trajectory, thereby helping reduce the instability that may arise when growing-season start and end dates are determined solely by phenological rules.

Based on this framework, this study constructed GlobalRice500, a global 500~m daily rice planting area dataset for 2001--2022. 
Using multiple reference data sources, including high-resolution rice maps from typical reference regions worldwide, visually interpreted samples, and country-scale statistics from the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO), the reliability of the dataset was evaluated from two perspectives: pixel-level spatial identification accuracy and country-scale area consistency. The results show that GlobalRice500 achieved overall accuracies of 88.85\%--99.60\% in nine typical reference regions worldwide, and maintained high consistency with FAO rice statistical areas for 113 countries during 2001--2022. These results indicate that GlobalRice500 can support long-term analyses of rice spatiotemporal dynamics at global, continental, and national scales.
Based on GlobalRice500, this study further revealed changes in the global rice planting pattern since the beginning of the 21st century. 
From 2001 to 2022, the global rice harvested area increased from approximately 138 million hectares to approximately 159 million hectares, with a net increase of more than 21 million hectares. 
Asia has consistently been the dominant region of global rice harvested area, accounting for more than 85\% of the global total over the long term and contributing 70.7\% of the global net increase. Rice area in Africa increased from approximately 7.7 million hectares to approximately 15.4 million hectares, making it the fastest-growing continent; its positive increase was equivalent to 39.4\% of the global net increase. 
At the national scale, India and China continued to form the dual core of the global rice planting pattern. The increase in rice area and the rise in ranking of countries such as Nigeria and Cambodia indicate that global rice expansion is extending toward emerging rice-growing regions in parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

% Temperature
On the basis of the global long-term daily extraction of rice planting areas, this study further combined a global 1~km daily land surface temperature (LST) dataset for 2003--2020 to construct a comparative analysis framework between paddy fields and surrounding non-rice croplands. The difference between non-rice cropland LST and paddy-field LST was defined as $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$, which was used to quantify the land-surface temperature effect of paddy fields relative to adjacent croplands. 
The results show that global paddy fields exhibited a widespread and stable daytime local land-surface cooling effect during the rice growing season. The annual mean $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ ranged from 0.21 to 0.27~$^\circ$C, and the 95\% confidence intervals were above 0 in all years. 
Nighttime $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ was much smaller in magnitude than daytime $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ and was close to 0, indicating that the cooling effect of paddy fields mainly occurred during daytime energy partitioning processes driven by solar radiation, enhanced evapotranspiration, and reduced sensible heat flux.
The cooling effect of paddy fields showed a clear dependence on phenological stage. Daytime $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ first increased and then decreased as the rice growing season progressed, forming an inverted V-shaped pattern, and reached its peak around day of season (DOS) 60--100. The peak period corresponded to the stage of rapid rice canopy development and active evapotranspiration. 
Further analysis showed that the land-surface cooling effect of paddy fields was jointly regulated by landscape composition, spatial continuity of paddy-field patches, and boundary adjacency. 
At the 10~km grid scale, each 1\% increase in the local proportion of paddy fields increased daytime $\Delta \mathrm{LST}$ by approximately 0.0014~$^\circ$C on average. 
As the paddy field sizes increased from approximately 0.5~km to approximately 5.5~km, the daytime mean cooling increased from approximately 0.13~$^\circ$C to approximately 0.43~$^\circ$C, and the peak cooling within the growing season increased from approximately 0.18~$^\circ$C to approximately 0.69~$^\circ$C. 
The analysis based on Non-Paddy Ladders (NPLs) showed that the low-temperature signal of paddy fields could extend into adjacent non-rice croplands and gradually weakened with increasing distance from paddy-field boundaries. 
These results indicate that paddy-field cooling is a land-surface biophysical effect formed under the combined influence of field-surface water, canopy development, and landscape structure, with significant temporal stage dependence, scale dependence, and spatial spillover characteristics.

This study establishes a complete research chain from the development of a daily rice growing-season identification algorithm, to the construction of a global long-term daily rice planting area dataset, and further to the assessment of the land-surface climatic effects of paddy fields. 
The MPD\_DTW framework provides a phenologically interpretable technical framework for daily rice planting area mapping across regions and cropping systems. 
GlobalRice500 transforms this identification capability into a long-term, continuous, and reusable global data basis for rice dynamics, and provides key support for assessing the land-surface temperature effects of paddy fields under the constraint of the actual rice growing season. 
The assessment of the land-surface cooling effect of paddy fields reveals the biophysical regulation of rice ecosystems on the land-surface thermal environment beyond greenhouse gas emissions. This study extends paddy fields from static crop distribution objects to dynamic land-system units with identifiable growing-season processes and quantifiable land-surface climatic effects. It helps establish a more complete understanding of the climatic effects of rice ecosystems across food production, water-resource use, greenhouse gas emissions, and local thermal-environment regulation, and provides a data basis and scientific evidence for sustainable management and climate adaptation research in agricultural land systems.

\noindent \textbf{Keywords}: paddy rice; remote sensing; GlobalRice500; MPD\_DTW; MODIS; land surface temperature; land surface cooling effect
