\chapternonum{摘要}

全球水稻种植区既关系粮食安全，也深刻影响气候系统。稻田是大气甲烷的重要人为排放源，其温室气体增温效应已得到广泛研究；然而，稻田水层和植被蒸散发所产生的地表生物物理降温效应却长期缺乏系统认识。在全球变暖与粮食安全双重压力下，全面评估稻田对气候系统的双重影响具有重要的科学意义。

针对全球水稻种植区气候背景多样、播栽期不固定、生长季长度差异大和复种制度复杂等难题，本文提出融合移动物候检测与动态时间规整（Dynamic Time Warping, DTW）曲线匹配策略的 MPD\_DTW 框架，构建能够适应全球复杂水稻种植制度的日尺度遥感识别方法。该方法将水稻移栽或返青初期的淹水物候先验与生长季内植被指数曲线形态匹配相结合，通过逐像元、逐日搜索水稻生长季起始日（SOS）和结束日（EOS），提高了全球不同稻区间的时序适应性和方法可解释性。基于该框架，本文研制了 2001--2022 年全球 500 m 日尺度水稻种植区数据集 GlobalRice500，提供全球主要稻区的年度水稻分布及逐像元生长季信息。

基于 GlobalRice500，本文揭示了 2001--2022 年全球水稻种植区的长期时空变化，并在真实水稻生长季约束下系统评估了全球稻田地表温度（LST）相对于周边非稻田农田的差异。结果表明，2001--2022 年全球水稻面积整体呈波动上升趋势，22 年间净增加超过 2100 万公顷；亚洲长期占全球水稻面积的 85\% 以上，非洲是增长最快的大洲。全球稻田在生长季白天具有广泛的局地降温效应，平均降温约 0.21--0.27 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$，降温强度在分蘖--抽穗期达到峰值，而夜间 $\Delta$LST 接近 0，表明稻田降温主要源于白天太阳辐射驱动下的蒸散发增强和能量分配调整。进一步分析发现，稻田降温强度受稻田比例、田块规模和空间外溢效应显著调控：稻田比例每增加 1\%，降温幅度约增加 0.0014 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$；大田块平均降温幅度可达 0.69 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$，显著高于小田块的 0.18 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$；稻田降温效应不仅局限于稻田内部，还可向周边非稻田区域产生空间外溢。

高时空分辨率全球稻田动态数据集，是认识稻田地表生物物理气候效应的基础。本研究形成了方法创新、数据产品、全球变化规律和地表气候效应相互支撑的完整研究体系，为理解全球稻田如何被识别、如何变化以及如何影响地表气候提供了新的科学证据。

\chapternonum{Abstract}

Global rice cultivation areas are closely linked to both food security and the climate system. Rice paddies are a significant anthropogenic source of atmospheric methane, and their greenhouse gas warming effects have been extensively studied; however, the biophysical surface cooling effects resulting from paddy water layers and vegetation evapotranspiration remain insufficiently understood. Under the dual pressures of global warming and food security, comprehensively assessing the dual climate impacts of rice paddies is of great scientific significance.

To address the challenges posed by diverse climatic backgrounds, variable planting dates, different growing-season lengths, and complex multiple-cropping systems across global rice cultivation areas, this study proposes the MPD\_DTW framework, which integrates moving phenological detection with dynamic time warping (DTW) curve matching to construct a daily-scale remote sensing identification method adaptable to complex global rice cropping systems. This method combines phenological prior knowledge of flooding during rice transplanting or early regreening with the shape matching of vegetation-index curves during the growing season, and searches pixel by pixel and day by day for the start of season (SOS) and end of season (EOS). Based on this framework, we develop GlobalRice500, a global 500-m daily rice cultivation dataset for 2001--2022, providing annual rice distribution and pixel-level growing-season information for major rice-producing regions worldwide.

Based on GlobalRice500, this study reveals the long-term spatiotemporal changes in global rice cultivation areas from 2001 to 2022 and systematically evaluates land surface temperature (LST) differences between paddy rice and surrounding non-rice farmland under the constraint of actual rice growing seasons. The results show that global rice area exhibited an overall fluctuating upward trend from 2001 to 2022, with a net increase of more than 21 million hectares. Asia consistently accounted for more than 85\% of global rice area, while Africa was the fastest-growing continent. Global rice paddies exhibit a widespread daytime local cooling effect during the growing season, with an average cooling of approximately 0.21--0.27 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ and peak cooling intensity during the tillering--heading stage. Nighttime $\Delta$LST is close to zero, indicating that paddy cooling is mainly driven by daytime evapotranspiration enhancement and energy-partitioning adjustment under solar radiation. Further analyses show that cooling intensity is significantly regulated by paddy proportion, field size, and spatial spillover effects: each 1\% increase in paddy proportion corresponds to an approximately 0.0014 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ enhancement in cooling magnitude; large fields exhibit an average cooling of approximately 0.69 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$, substantially higher than the 0.18 $^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ observed for small fields; moreover, the cooling effect extends beyond paddy field boundaries into adjacent non-rice areas.

High-spatiotemporal-resolution global paddy rice dynamic datasets are the fundamental basis for understanding the biophysical climate effects of rice paddies. By linking methodological innovation, data production, global change patterns, and surface climate effects, this study provides new scientific evidence for understanding how global rice paddies can be identified, how they have changed, and how they affect the land surface climate.
